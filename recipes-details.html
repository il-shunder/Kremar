Philadelphia roll with edible gold
Ingredients:
1 cup of rice (better to use sushi rice)
8 tablespoons of sesame seeds
8–10 sheets nori seaweed
100 g of salmon
2 cucumbers
4 salad leaves
1 chili pepper
1 Philadelphia cheese
4 tablespoons of rice or apple cider vinegar
gold leaf
2 teaspoons of wasabi
soy sauce + wasabi to serve
Cooking
1.
Wash the rice, pour the boiling water (2 glasses), simmer until al dente.
2.
Add 2 tablespoons of vinegar, stir, and cook until tender. The rice should be quite sticky. Let cool slightly, add sesame seeds (6 tablespoons), stir.
3.
Rinse and dry the vegetables. Cut salmon, cucumbers, lettuce and peppers lengthwise into thin strips. Place in different containers.
4.
In a deep bowl of water add the remaining vinegar and 2 teaspoons of wasabi, place the nori seaweed in it.
5.
Prepare a board where you're going to put the rolls, a mat for curling. Place a gold leaf on the bamboo mat and a sheet of seaweed on top, shiny side down.
6.
On the half of the seaweed sheet closest to you, place the rice (about 3 tablespoons), spreading it evenly over this part of the sheet. Squeeze a strip of wasabi in the middle of the rice. Also place lettuce, cheese, peppers, cucumber, and salmon lengthwise.
7.
Roll up the roll tightly, moisten the finishing edge of the seaweed with vinegar, thus 'gluing' it. If necessary, adjust the shape of the roll with a mat. Roll up the rest of the rolls in the same way, cut each into 8 equal pieces with a sharp knife and garnish with sesame seeds and/or olives before serving.
8.
Serve with soy sauce and wasabi.
Enjoy!
